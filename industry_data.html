As the IT environment has expanded further into the professional world, becoming inextricably embedded in almost all facets of modern business, the ability for the IT professional to be able to effectively communicate has become essential. This is borne out by the data, suggesting communications skills are the most valuable, at almost three times the second ranked skill of problem solving. The ability to convey and translate concepts and needs across a variety of stakeholders is valuable in any profession, and further developing these will pay dividends regardless of my future career path.

The role I chose for my ideal job as part of Assignment 1 did not require expertise with several the middle ranked specific skill listed in the Burning Glass Data. That said, integrating with existing business processes, by providing SAP compatibility or other legacy software is likely to increase the value of any development I may be part of. There was also no direct expectation of proficiency with business management of analysis, however these skills may become more valuable as I advance my career and take on roles where consideration of the needs of the business become important.

As the roles I considered were entry level, the expectation for Leadership skills, Quality Assurance and Time Management was low. The skills will increase in relevance as I progress through my career, taking on more senior positions.

After reviewing the Burning Glass Data, I would still pursue the career path I originally chose. While there are specific skills which I may have less opportunity to develop, I will have an opportunity to gain a broad spectrum of valuable skills, either through direct use (SQL, JavaScript, etc.) or indirectly through exposure to the professional environment (Business Management & Analysis, Building Relationships, etc.).
