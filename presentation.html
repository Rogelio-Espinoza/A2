Water cannot live without it.

Drinking Water comes from many sources, such as Rivers, Lakes, Tanks and of course your kitchen tap

On an average day, an average person will drink an average amount of 2 and a half liters of water.

Some people drink more.

According to the World Health Organization 2 billion people do not have access to safe drinking water. These water sources are contaminated with poo, bathing people, dirty wash and dead animals.

Drinking these water sources are linked to Transmission of diseases such as Diarrhea, Hepatitis A, Typhoid and Polio.

An estimate of 1 million people die each year from Diarrhea as a result of unsafe drinking water, sanitation and personal hygiene.

In Australia we are lucky to have safe drinking water from town mains, It's something we take for granted.

But for Individuals in rural areas, they may not have access to town mains, and have to relie on Rainwater, which is stored in Water Tanks.

Tank water is generally safe to Drink, there are times where the water quality demissisions quickly.

Bird and Animal droppings on the Roof of a house can make its way into the WaterTank

I've seen Dead possums, lizards' birds inside water tanks.

These contaminations can cause harmful microorganisms, such as Bactria and Parasite

With that we came up with the idea of a Water Tank Sampler, a device that attaches inline and gives the user a quick reading to decide if the water source is safe enough to drink. It doesnâ€™t clean the tank for you but does give a reference to determine if the water is still safe to drink

The device attaches inline on the water tanks outlet. It senses, PH Levels, Electrical Conductivity, Temperature, and a camera to find water soluble. Is the device of the future, to help determine if the Tank water source is safe to drink. And that the parameters have not changed.

The device uses a a simple traffic light system, helps you quickly determine if the Tank water is still safe to drink.

The device also has a web interface to see the Status of all the sensors to get a more in-depth readout.

In the future a history log will be implemented to see the change of the water over time.

The device is simple, all you need to do is connect the device inline of the water source and find a reliable power source to power the electronics, a simple battery and solar panel setup will be sufficed.

Connecting the device to the internet is also simple, follow the basic instructions that will be supplied with the device to connect it to the Internet.
